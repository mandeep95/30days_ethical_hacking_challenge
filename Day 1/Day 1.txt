Day -1 Learn Ethical Hacking 

 Topic - Penetration Testing 

a cybersecurity practice that involves simulating cyberattacks on computer systems, networks, or applications to identify vulnerabilities and weaknesses.

1. Purpose of Penetration Testing:

Penetration testing aims to evaluate the security of a system by attempting to exploit vulnerabilities.
It helps organizations understand their security posture and assess potential risks.
The ultimate goal is to improve security by identifying and addressing weaknesses.

2. Types of Penetration Testing:

Black Box Testing: Testers have no prior knowledge of the target system.
White Box Testing: Testers have full knowledge of the target system's architecture and source code.
Gray Box Testing: Testers have partial knowledge of the target system.

3. Common Penetration Testing Tools:

Tools like Nmap, Metasploit, Burp Suite, and Wireshark are commonly used by penetration testers.
